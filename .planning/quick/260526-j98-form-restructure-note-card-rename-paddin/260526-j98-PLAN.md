### Item 3 — Trim bottom whitespace to ~32dp

**Root cause** (`manual_one_step_screen.dart:337-411`):
- Line 337: `final smartKeypadHeight = _computeSmartKeypadHeight(context);` returns the FULL height of the smart keypad (~40% screen height + paddings) regardless of whether the keypad is currently visible.
- Line 340: `scrollPaddingBottom = math.max(viewInsetsBottom, smartKeypadHeight)` → always at least the full keypad height.
- Line 407-412: this padding is applied to the form's `SingleChildScrollView.padding.bottom`.

Result: when the form content is short, it scrolls within a viewport that reserves ~40% screen height of bottom padding, leaving the visible huge white gap between the last card and the keypad. This was intentional during P19 to prevent content being hidden by the keypad when content is taller than the viewport, but for a short form it produces dead space.

**Target behavior**:
- Replace the `smartKeypadHeight` reservation with a fixed comfortable bottom inset of `32.0` dp (per user decision).
- Keep `viewInsetsBottom` term so IME does not occlude content when a TextField is focused: `scrollPaddingBottom = math.max(viewInsetsBottom, 32.0)`.
- The SmartKeyboard itself sits AFTER the `Expanded` in the outer `Column` (line 434 `AnimatedSlide(... SmartKeyboard ...)`), so it naturally bounds the scrollable area from below — the bottom padding only needs to clear IME, not the SmartKeyboard.
- Delete `_computeSmartKeypadHeight` (and the helper math at lines 319-328) once `smartKeypadHeight` has no remaining caller — verify no other reference exists (grep first).

This is the minimal, root-cause-targeted fix. NOT adding any `Align(topCenter)` or `mainAxisSize` workaround.

### Item 4 — Restore SmartKeyboard focus after picker dismissal
